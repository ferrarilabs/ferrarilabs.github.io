What I do

Advisory work across financial crime transformation, model risk, and the practical design of controls that hold up under regulator scrutiny. I spend most of my time in the space between what leadership intends and what investigators can actually execute.

How I work

I focus on the hard edges: broken operating models, explainability gaps, fragmented data, false-positive fatigue, weak escalation paths, and architecture that looks defensible on paper but fails when examined. I also build — quietly — because some problems are not being solved well enough by legacy approaches.

About

Advisory-led.
Technically grounded.

My work sits at the intersection of compliance, technology, and operational execution. I have spent years helping institutions make better decisions in environments where the cost of being wrong is high — suspicious activity detection, sanctions exposure, onboarding risk, model challenge, and digital asset oversight.

That perspective comes from seeing the same pattern repeatedly. The issue is rarely just the model, or the rule, or the platform. It is the gap between what leadership expects, what investigators can actually work with, what the data supports, and what regulators will ultimately question.

I work at senior levels — with CROs, BSA Officers, CIOs, and their teams — where the conversations involve both regulatory posture and the systems underneath it. The technical depth is what makes the advisory useful.

I also build. Some recurring problems are not being solved well enough by existing tooling. When that is the case, advisory alone is not sufficient.

Focus areas

Where I tend to be most useful.

01

AML Transformation

Assessment, redesign, and modernization of transaction monitoring programs. Operating model evaluation, alert logic, case management, and regulator-facing documentation. Includes governance and escalation path design.

02

Model Validation

Independent challenge of detection models, scoring logic, and thresholds. Explainability, backtesting, documentation quality, and defensibility under OCC 2011-12 and NYDFS expectations. This is where programs quietly break.

03

AI in Compliance

Practical application of AI and machine learning where auditability, traceability, and controlled decisioning matter as much as detection performance. Governance design, vendor evaluation, and exam posture preparation.

04

Digital Asset Risk

Crypto compliance architecture, blockchain analytics integration, Travel Rule/TRISA, VASP exposure mapping, and sanctions screening for on-chain activity. Built for institutions already operating in the space and those preparing to.

05

Sanctions & Screening

Program design, watch list coverage, name matching logic, correspondent banking exposure, and controls for cross-border and digital asset flows. Includes OFAC SDN, UN, EU, and OFSI alignment.

06

KYC / CDD / EDD

Onboarding controls, customer risk scoring, beneficial ownership, enhanced due diligence workflows, and the data architecture needed to sustain them. Particular focus on crypto-native and high-risk entity segments.
